# Demo Script

## Goal

Show that MemeScope AI is a polished, useful, and reliable AI product — not a chatbot wrapper.

## Demo length

60–90 seconds

## Ideal script

### 1. Open strong (10s)

> "This is MemeScope AI — an AI copilot for researching meme tokens and internet-native projects. Instead of giving you a vague chat answer, it turns messy inputs into a structured research report in one screen."

### 2. Show the input flexibility (10s)

- On the homepage, point to the form.
- Mention that users can paste:
  - token name
  - ticker
  - contract
  - website
  - X handle
  - manual notes

> "This matters because meme projects are often incomplete and noisy. The product still works even when data is missing."

### 3. Run a benchmark example (15s)

- Click **BONK benchmark**.
- Hit **Generate research report**.
- While loading, mention:

> "MemeScope normalizes the input, pulls any available market and website context, then generates a structured report with confidence and missing-data notices."

### 4. Walk the output (25s)

Call out these sections quickly:

- executive summary
- confidence score
- bull case vs bear case
- risk flags
- narrative / meme angle
- diligence checklist
- launch-ready post copy

Suggested line:

> "The key idea is that it does both research and communication. You get the diligence view and the presentation layer in the same product."

### 5. Show demo reliability (10s)

- Click **Copy share link** or **Download JSON**.
- Mention the fallback design.

> "If a live API is slow or missing, the app degrades gracefully instead of breaking. That makes it much safer for a hackathon demo."

### 6. Close with the value prop (10s)

> "MemeScope helps traders, creators, and communities understand what a project is, what could go right, what could go wrong, and how to talk about it — in under 30 seconds."

## Backup flow if live enrichment is sparse

Use **Manual-only concept**.

Say:

> "Even with only manual notes and no strong live token match, MemeScope still gives a structured research memo instead of failing."

## What not to do during the demo

- Do not live-edit environment variables.
- Do not depend on obscure tokens with ambiguous tickers.
- Do not scroll too much.
- Do not explain every field.
- Do not claim the app proves safety or investment quality.
